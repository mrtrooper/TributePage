Chestor Bennington

"The man who saved lives through music"

Chester Bennington, performing the song "Given Up" live at Download festival, New York

  • 1976: Born in Phoenix, Arizona.
  • 1998: Bennington quit his day job at a digital services firm and took his family to California, where he had a successful audition with Linkin Park
  • 2000: Linkin Park released their debut album, Hybrid Theory, through Warner Bros. Records which was certified diamond by the RIAA in 2005
  • 2003: The band's second album, Meteora, reached number one on the Billboard 200 album chart, as did its third album, Minutes to Midnight (2007).
  • Awards: 2 Grammy Awards, 6 American Music Awards, 4 MTV Video Music Awards and 3 World Music Awards.
  • 2003: MTV2 named Linkin Park the 6th greatest band of the music video era and the 3rd best of the new millennium. Billboard ranked Linkin Park No. 19 on the Best Artists of the Decade chart.
  • 2012: The band was voted as the greatest artist of the 2000s in a Bracket Madness poll on VH1
  • 2017: Bennington was found dead by his housekeeper at his home in Palos Verdes Estates, California. His death was ruled a suicide by hanging.

"You can't be afraid of the people willing to hurt you, cause if you fear life, you'll never live."-Chestor Bennington
